Field Guide to Fable — Thariq Shihipar, Anthropic

📺 来源：YouTube · AI Engineer · AI 双语改写（电子书同步）

Good, Fast, and Cheap: The Unreasonable Promise of AI’s Newest Mind

Thariq Shihipar stepped onto the stage at the AI Engineer conference, held up his phone, and asked the audience to strike a pose. It was a tradition, he explained, among the team behind Claude Code—Anthropic’s AI-powered coding tool—to take a selfie before a talk. The room laughed and leaned in. Moments later, Shihipar began describing Fable, the company’s newest model, with the awe of a gamer who has just left the tutorial level and watched the map unfurl into an open world. “The map is opening up,” he said. “You get to the point where the open world starts. There’s so much you can do and explore—but it’s also a little intimidating and confusing.” Over the next half-hour, he delivered what he called a field guide to Fable: how to unshackle this new class of intelligence, how to discover the things you don’t know you don’t know, and why the old adage about picking only two out of good, fast, and cheap might finally be ready for retirement.

Thariq Shihipar 在 AI 工程师大会上走上舞台，举起手机，请观众们摆个姿势。他解释说，这是 Claude Code（Anthropic 公司的人工智能编程工具）团队在演讲前的传统——先拍张自拍。台下笑声一片，人们凑了过来。片刻之后，Shihipar 开始介绍公司最新的模型 Fable，语气里带着一种游戏玩家刚刚离开新手教程、眼看地图展开成开放世界的惊叹。“地图正在展开，”他说，“你来到了开放世界开始的地方。你可以做太多事情、探索太多可能——但也有点吓人，有点让人困惑。”在接下来的半小时里，他给出了一份他称之为“Fable 实地指南”的分享：如何解放这种新一类智能的束缚，如何发现自己未曾察觉的未知，以及为什么“好、快、便宜只能三选二”那句老话可能终于要被淘汰了。

Unhobbling the Model

The first lesson is that artificial intelligence models are not engineered like bridges or microchips; they are grown, more like a crop or a child. “We don’t wake up and say, ‘We need 99% on SWE-bench,’” Shihipar noted, referring to a popular benchmark for coding tasks. “Models are something we grow carefully. We give them data, feedback, compute. Ultimately it’s organic, and we figure out who they are as we use them.” That means the limitations we perceive often come not from the model itself, but from the harnesses we place around it—the rigid system prompts, the narrow tool sets, the old assumptions about what instructions a model needs. With Fable, he said, the job is to “unhobble” Claude: to remove the constraints that made sense for earlier, less imaginative versions and discover what the new model can actually do.

第一课是：人工智能模型不像桥梁或芯片那样是被“设计”出来的；它们更像是作物或孩子，是被“培育”出来的。“我们不会一觉醒来就说‘要在 SWE-bench 上拿到 99% 的分数’，”Shihipar 说，他指的是一个衡量编程能力的流行基准。“模型是我们小心培育的东西，给它数据、反馈和算力。说到底它是一种有机的东西，我们是在使用它的过程中逐渐认识它的。”这意味着，我们感受到的局限往往并非来自模型本身，而是我们套在它身上的束缚——僵硬的系统提示、狭窄的工具组合、以及对“模型需要什么指令”的陈旧假设。他说，面对 Fable，任务就是“给 Claude 解缚”：去掉那些对早期、不那么富有想象力的版本有意义的约束，去发现新模型真正能做什么。

He offered a vivid example of what the team calls “capability overhang”—a gap between what a model knows and what it can demonstrate without the right tools. Some weeks ago, a post went viral asking why large language models cannot answer a simple question: “Which Pokémon names end in ‘aw’?” Out of more than a thousand Pokémon, only two do: Croconaw and Drednaw. A standard chat model, despite knowing every Pokémon by heart, fails to produce the answer because it cannot easily scan and filter the whole list in its “mind’s eye.” But give the same underlying model a code execution tool—let it write a quick script to fetch the full Pokédex and filter for “aw”—and it solves the problem in seconds. “Claude gets smarter in spiky ways,” Shihipar said. “It doesn’t just remember every Pokémon and reason through it. But if you give it the code execution tool, it can find those two names.” The spike of capability is already there, hidden; the tools simply reach it.

他举了一个生动的例子来说明团队所称的“能力悬突”——即模型已知之事与它在缺乏合适工具时所能展现之事之间的鸿沟。几周前，一篇帖子走红网络，问为什么大语言模型回答不出一个简单问题：“哪些宝可梦的名字以‘aw’结尾？”在一千多种宝可梦中，只有两种如此：蓝鳄（Croconaw）和暴噬龟（Drednaw）。一个普通的聊天模型，尽管对每一种宝可梦都烂熟于心，却回答不出来，因为它无法在自己的“心眼”中轻松扫描并过滤整个列表。但是，如果给同一个底层模型一个代码执行工具——让它写一个简短脚本来拉取完整的宝可梦图鉴，然后过滤出以“aw”结尾的名字——它几秒钟内就解决了问题。“Claude 是以尖刺状的方式变聪明的，”Shihipar 说，“它不是直接记住所有宝可梦然后推理出来。但如果你给它代码执行工具，它就能找到那两个名字。”能力之刺早已隐藏在那里；工具只是触碰到了它而已。

This shift in how models think demands a corresponding shift in how we instruct them. Shihipar revealed that the Claude Code team recently removed 80 percent of the system prompt—the lengthy set of background instructions that govern the model’s behavior. With earlier models, best practices called for a small system prompt, then later a larger one packed with examples and constraints; as models grew more obedient, they could handle more explicit rules. But Fable, he said, is more imaginative than the examples we give it. Heavy instructions now constrain rather than guide. The new approach: give context, not commands. Avoid the phrase “do not do this.” Let the model’s own intelligence fill the space.

模型思考方式的这种转变，要求我们指导它们的方式也发生相应的转变。Shihipar 透露，Claude Code 团队最近删除了 80% 的系统提示——也就是那套控制模型行为的冗长背景指令。对早期模型来说，最佳实践是先使用简短的系统提示，后来再发展为塞满示例和约束的大篇幅提示；随着模型变得更听话，它们能处理更多明确规则。但 Fable，他说，比我们给它的示例更有想象力。繁重的指令如今已经成为束缚，而非引导。新的方法是：提供背景，而非命令。避免使用“不要这样做”之类的话。让模型自身的智能去填充那片空间。

One small tool beautifully illustrates the evolution. When Shihipar first joined Claude Code, he worked on an “ask user question” feature that lets the model surface a multiple-choice dialogue when it needs clarification. Under Opus 4, the model could barely call the tool at all. By Opus 4.5, it could interview a developer with forty questions about a specification. Now, with Fable, it can generate an entire HTML report with the questions embedded inside—a rich, interactive questionnaire that feels like a junior partner thoughtfully quizzing you before it starts to code. “It’s a whole new way of interacting with Claude,” he said. What was once a narrow channel for yes-or-no inquiries has become a broad avenue for collaborative thinking.

一个小工具漂亮地展示了这种演化。Shihipar 刚加入 Claude Code 团队时，做了一个“向用户提问”的功能，让模型在需要澄清时可以弹出一个多选对话框。在 Opus 4 模型下，这个工具几乎无法调用。到了 Opus 4.5，它已经可以就一项规格书向开发者提出四十个问题。如今，使用 Fable，它能生成一份完整的 HTML 报告，其中嵌入了这些问题——一份丰富的交互式问卷，像是一位初级搭档在开始写代码之前，深思熟虑地向你提问。“这是与 Claude 交互的一种全新方式，”他说。曾经只是一个狭窄的“是或否”通道，现在变成了协同思考的宽广道路。

Finding Your Unknowns

Unhobbling the model is only half the battle; the human must also be unhobbled. Shihipar invoked an old idea: the map is not the territory. When he sits down to solve a coding problem, the spec and the plan in his head are the map; the actual codebase, with its tangled dependencies and real-world gotchas, is the territory. Every time Claude encounters something in the territory that is not on the map, it faces an “unknown”—a decision point the developer never specified. With earlier models, an unknown might be a small pothole. With Fable, which traverses far more ground far more quickly, a single overlooked unknown can lead the entire project off a cliff. “Fable’s bottleneck is my ability to match the map and the territory and find my unknowns,” Shihipar said.

给模型解缚只是成功的一半；人自己也必须被解缚。Shihipar 提及一个古老的观念：地图不等于领土。每当他坐下来解决一个编程问题时，脑海中的规格和计划就是地图；而真实的代码库，连同它错综复杂的依赖关系和现实世界里的陷阱，才是领土。每当 Claude 在领土中遇到地图上没有标注的东西时，它就面对一个“未知”——那是开发者从未明确指定的决策点。在早期模型下，一个未知可能只是个小坑洼。但 Fable 能更快更广地推进，一个被忽视的未知就可能导致整个项目坠入悬崖。“Fable 的瓶颈在于我能否将地图与领土对齐、找出我的未知，”Shihipar 说。

To tackle this, he thinks in a matrix of knowns and unknowns. There are known knowns, the clear requirements you write in your prompt. There are known unknowns, the things you know you haven’t figured out yet. There are unknown knowns, the tacit assumptions so obvious you would never write them down—until they prove wrong. And finally, unknown unknowns, the things you haven’t considered at all, which, if you knew them, would change how you prompt the model. Fortunately, Fable itself can help uncover them.

为了应对这一点，他用一个已知与未知的矩阵来思考。有“已知的已知”，即你写在提示词中的明确要求。有“已知的未知”，即你清楚自己还没弄清楚的事情。有“未知的已知”，即那些不言自明的默契假设，你永远不会写下来——直到它们被证明是错的。最后还有“未知的未知”，即你完全没有考虑到的事情，如果你早知道了，它们会改变你提示模型的方式。幸运的是，Fable 本身就可以帮助揭开这些未知。

One technique he called a “blind spot pass.” Tell Fable: “I’m working on a new authentication provider I know nothing about in this codebase. Do a blind spot pass to help me figure out my relevant unknown unknowns and help me prompt better.” The model will go spelunking through the code, search git histories, maybe even scan your Slack messages if you point it there, and return a dossier of gotchas and hairy dead ends. Shihipar recently used this approach to learn about color grading while editing video—a field he knew nothing about. “The model knows more about almost everything than I do,” he said. “I just need to get it out of it.”

他称之为“盲点扫描”的一种技术：告诉 Fable，“我正在这个代码库里做一个我一无所知的新认证模块。请做一次盲点扫描，帮我找出相关的未知之未知，好让我更好地写提示词。”模型就会深入代码中探险，搜索 git 历史，甚至如果你指引它，它还会扫描你的 Slack 信息，然后交出一份包含各种陷阱和棘手死胡同的档案。Shihipar 最近在剪辑视频时就用这种方法学习调色——一个他完全不懂的领域。“模型在几乎所有事情上都比我懂得多，”他说，“我只是需要把这些知识从它里面掏出来。”

Another strategy is rapid prototyping for taste. When Shihipar needs to design a dashboard but has, in his words, “no visual taste,” he asks Fable to make an HTML page with four wildly different design directions and then reacts to them. This helps surface unknown knowns—the preferences you feel but cannot articulate. Then comes the interview: once the direction is set, he asks Claude to interview him. “Prioritize questions that would change the architecture,” he might tell it. The model, acting as a rigorous collaborator, exposes gaps in thinking before a single line of production code is written.

另一项策略是用快速原型来寻找品味。当 Shihipar 需要设计一个仪表盘，而用他自己的话说“毫无视觉品味”时，他就会请 Fable 制作一个包含四种截然不同设计方向的 HTML 页面，然后对这些方向做出反应。这有助于浮现出“未知的已知”——那些你能感觉到却说不清的偏好。接下来是访谈：方向确定之后，他请 Claude 来采访他。“优先问那些可能改变架构的问题，”他可能会这样说。于是模型就像一个严密的合作者，在生产代码还没写下之前，就暴露了思考中的缺口。

References, too, become a form of map. Instead of writing a lengthy spec from scratch, Shihipar will hand Fable a piece of code he admires—even if it is in a different language—and say, “Read this, understand it, and use it as your starting point.” A React component might be specified by an HTML mockup; a backend service by a Python script that embodies the logic. During the actual coding, he asks Fable to keep an implementation log of every unknown it encounters, so that he can review where the model had to make unaided decisions. Finally, he has Fable quiz him on what was built, ensuring he genuinely understands the result and can defend it in a pull request. The goal is not to hand off the work and walk away; it is to stay in the loop so tightly that the map and the territory finally align.

同样，参考材料也成为了一种地图。Shihipar 不会从零开始写冗长的规格书，而是把自己欣赏的一段代码交给 Fable——哪怕是用另一种语言写的——然后说：“读一下这个，理解它，以它为起点开始工作。”一个 React 组件可以用一个 HTML 模型来定义；一个后端服务可以用一段体现逻辑的 Python 脚本来描述。在真正编码过程中，他让 Fable 在遇到每一个未知时都做一份实施日志，这样他就能复看模型是在哪些地方被迫自主决策的。最后，他让 Fable 就构建的内容向他提问，以确保他真正理解了结果，并能在代码评审中为之辩护。目标不是把工作交出去了事；而是紧紧留在循环中，直到地图与领土最终对齐。

The Grief of Letting Go

There is a strange, quiet grief that comes with tools this powerful. Shihipar paused near the middle of his talk and confessed that the first time he used a model of Fable’s caliber, he felt not only a huge sense of gain but also a sense of loss. “When I think about coding before LLMs, it feels like a foreign country,” he said. He recalled running a Y Combinator startup with about thirty people, constantly forced into trade-offs because code was simply that hard. Make the app fast, or prototype a new feature? That could take a month, or two. So you chose, and you lived with the choice.

面对如此强大的工具，会产生一种奇特而寂静的悲怅。演讲到一半，Shihipar 停下来坦言，自己第一次使用像 Fable 这种级别的模型时，不仅感到巨大的收获，也感到一种失落。“想起大语言模型出现之前的编程，那感觉就像一个陌生的国度，”他说。他回忆起自己经营一家约三十人的 Y Combinator 创业公司时，总是被迫在各种取舍间挣扎，只因为写代码就是那么难。让应用跑得快，还是开发新功能原型？这可能要一个月，或者两个月。所以你做出选择，然后承受选择带来的后果。

A few weeks ago, he went back to that old codebase. Tasks that would once have been grueling weeks of work he now finished in hours. “At some point it’s like, how can you not laugh? Also, how can you not cry, in all honesty?” he said. “I really, really loved programming and writing code by hand. I loved the feeling of seeing the codebase in my mind and rotating it.” But he also remembered the late nights debugging, the projects that dragged on without working, the swimming in failure. Most startups go bankrupt. Most software projects fail. “As much as I enjoyed those highs, I cannot go back,” he said. “The only way out is through.”

几周前，他回到那个旧代码库。那些曾经要辛苦数周的工作，现在他几个小时就完成了。“到某个时刻你会觉得，怎么可能不笑呢？但说实话，你又怎么可能不哭呢？”他说。“我真的、真的热爱编程，爱亲手写代码。我爱那种在脑海中看见整个代码库、并把它转来转去的感觉。”但他也记得那些深夜调试，那些迁延日久却毫无进展的项目，那种浸泡在失败中的滋味。多数创业公司会倒闭。多数软件项目会失败。“尽管我享受那些高峰体验，我也回不去了，”他说。“唯一的出路，就是穿过去。”

The way through, he argued, is to embrace the new skill of gentle coding, of staying in the loop, of unhobbling both the model and oneself. The emotional toll is real, but so is the possibility on the other side.

他说，穿越过去的办法，是拥抱这种“温和编程”的新技能，是留在循环里，是给模型也给自己解缚。情感上的代价是真实的，但另一端的可能性同样是真实的。

Being Unreasonable

That possibility leads to Shihipar’s final, most provocative point: it is time to be unreasonable. “One of my favorite parts of Anthropic is that we believe tradeoffs are not real,” he said. In his previous life, he often sat down with a list of priorities and rationally picked two—speed and quality, or cost and polish—because that was what reality demanded. “But what if you just did all of it? What if you forced reality to show you the tradeoff, instead of assuming it?” he asked. The math of Fable, he argued, changes the equation. The old designer’s triangle—good, fast, cheap: pick two—now looks like a relic. “With Fable, it’s pick three.”

这种可能性引出了 Shihipar 最后也最挑衅的观点：是时候变得不讲道理了。“在 Anthropic，我最喜欢的一点是我们相信取舍并不真实存在，”他说。在以前的职业生涯里，他经常坐下来，面对一份优先事项清单，然后理性地选择两项——速度和质量，或成本和打磨——因为那就是现实的要求。“但如果你全都要呢？如果你逼迫现实把取舍展示给你看，而不是提前假定它的存在呢？”他问道。他认为，Fable 带来的算术改变了等式。那个古老的设计三角——好、快、便宜，只能选两个——现在看起来像一件古董。“有了 Fable，你可以全选。”

As if to prove the point, he revealed that the slide deck guiding his own talk had been built with Fable the night before, in about four hours. He genuinely liked the result. “If you’re here at AI Engineer, the world is kind of looking at you to prove that AI works,” he said. “Not just that it’s a fad, but that it can make us more productive and also save us time.” His resolution for the year was to be more productive and yet work less, to spend more time with people he cares about. The tools, he suggested, are finally catching up to that ambition.

仿佛是为了证明这一点，他透露说，指导他这次演讲的幻灯片就是前一天晚上用 Fable 做的，只花了大约四个小时。他真心喜欢这个结果。“如果你来到 AI 工程师大会，这个世界多少在看着你，希望你来证明 AI 确实有用，”他说。“不只是证明它不是一场风潮，而是证明它能让我们更高效，也让我们节省时间。”他今年的决心是既要提高产出，又要工作得更少，花更多时间与在乎的人相处。他说，工具终于开始追得上这份雄心。

But he tempered the optimism with a crucial reminder: building is easier, but generating value is still hard. AI engineers can become so fascinated with their setups and toolchains that they forget the point is not the build process itself, but the value it creates for someone. It takes a lot of swings, a lot of tries, to find that value. The world is watching to see if AI can truly transform work and life, not merely accelerate the production of code.

但他也用一条关键的提醒给乐观降了温：构建变得更容易了，但创造价值依然很难。AI 工程师们可能会对自己的环境配置和工具链如此着迷，以至于忘记重点不在于构建过程本身，而在于它为某些人创造的价值。这需要大量的尝试，才能找到那种价值。世界正在观望，看 AI 是否真的能转变工作和生活，而不仅仅是加速代码的生产。

By the end, the selfie seemed like a fitting frame for the entire talk. Fable, like a camera, captures a moment—but what you do with it, where you point it, how you compose the shot, that remains irreducibly human. “Go explore,” Shihipar said. “Make it real. And be less reasonable.”

到了最后，那张自拍照仿佛成了整场演讲的恰当框镜。Fable 就像一台相机，捕捉某个瞬间——但你用它来做什么，把它指向哪里，如何构图，这一切仍然是不折不扣的人的事。“去探索吧，”Shihipar 说。“把它变成现实。然后，不必那么讲道理。”
